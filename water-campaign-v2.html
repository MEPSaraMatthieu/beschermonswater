Wat er gaande is

De mijnbouwlobby wil jouw waterbescherming afbreken

De Kaderrichtlijn Water beschermt al 25 jaar onze rivieren, meren en het grondwater van Europa. Ze is niet perfect — maar ze werkt.

De mijnbouwindustrie heeft de Commissie zwaar onder druk gezet om ze te herzien. De Commissie heeft nu een consultatie geopend — en op basis van die reacties zal ze beslissen of ze de wet al dan niet verzwakt.

Dat betekent: als genoeg mensen zich uitspreken, kunnen we de afzwakking tegenhouden. Maar dan moet het nu gebeuren. De deadline is volgende week dinsdag 14 april.

Lobbyisten beweren dat ze onze samenleving groener willen maken door meer grondstoffen op te graven. Maar je kan de ene milieucrisis niet oplossen door een andere te creëren. Het is de verantwoordelijkheid van mijnbouwbedrijven om te investeren in technologieën die schade voorkomen — niet om beschermingen af te bouwen.

The European Commission is considering weakening the law that protects your water. You can stop it.

I fight in the European Parliament for clean water for everyone. The Commission has not decided yet. But the deadline is approaching — and your voice counts.

The facts

Why this matters

⚗️

29%

Only 29% of European rivers, lakes and waterways are in good health. The law is already not being enforced enough.

🤝

108×

📋

2019

After a two-year evaluation the Commission itself said this law was fit for purpose. Updated just two months ago. And now suddenly a problem?

What's happening

The mining lobby wants to gut your water protection

The Water Framework Directive has protected Europe's rivers, lakes and groundwater for 25 years. It's not perfect — but it works.

The mining industry has put enormous pressure on the Commission to revise it. The Commission has now opened a consultation — and based on the responses, it will decide whether or not to weaken the law.

That means: if enough people speak up, this can be stopped. But it has to happen now. The deadline is 14 April.

They claim they want to make society greener by digging up more raw materials. But you cannot solve one environmental crisis by creating another. Mining companies must invest in cleaner technologies — not lobby to dismantle the protections that keep our water safe.

The Water Framework Directive has protected Europe's rivers, lakes and groundwater for 25 years. Today, only 29% of European rivers, lakes and waterways are in good health. The problem is not that this law is too strict — it is already not being sufficiently implemented and enforced. Weakening the Directive will not solve any problem; it will create new ones: more pollution, more risks to public health and nature, and less trust in European institutions. I call on the Commission not to revise or weaken the Water Framework Directive, but to prioritise its full implementation and enforcement. Clean water is not a luxury — it is a human right.

I am firmly opposed to any revision of the Water Framework Directive in favour of the mining industry. The mining industry held 108 meetings with the European Commission — lobbying with the Environment Commissioner increased by 220%. There is no evidence that the Water Framework Directive significantly hinders new mining projects. A revision is therefore not justified. The costs of weakening the law are passed on to taxpayers and nature. It is the responsibility of mining companies to invest in technologies that prevent harm. I urge the Commission not to reopen this law.

I am concerned about the possible revision of the Water Framework Directive. In 2019, after a two-year evaluation, the Commission concluded this law was fit for purpose. Two months ago it was updated. A new revision — without evidence that the law significantly blocks mining projects, and without thorough impact assessment — is premature and undermines trust in European institutions. Clean water is the foundation of our health, our food security and our economy. I urge the Commission not to reopen the Water Framework Directive, but to prioritise its full implementation and enforcement.

The Commission has not decided yet.

But the deadline is 14 April 2026. Your response can make the difference.
